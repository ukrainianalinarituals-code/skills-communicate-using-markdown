# Evening Ritual

Close your day with care. This evening ritual helps you transition from doing to being, and prepares your mind for restorative sleep.

## Overview

How you end your day shapes how you begin the next one. This 10–15 minute practice helps you:

- Process the events of the day
- Release tension and stress
- Set yourself up for quality rest

## The Practice

### 1. Digital Sunset (ongoing)

At least **30 minutes before bed**, put away screens. This is non-negotiable for a quality ritual.

### 2. Day Review (5 minutes)

Reflect on your day with gentle curiosity — not judgment.

Ask yourself:

- _What went well today?_
- _What would I do differently?_
- _What am I proud of, even if it's small?_

### 3. Release (3 minutes)

Write down anything that's still on your mind. Getting thoughts out of your head and onto paper clears mental space and reduces anxiety.

### 4. Gratitude Close (2 minutes)

End with **one thing** that made today worth it. It can be as simple as a good cup of tea or a kind word from a friend.

## Evening Checklist

- [ ] Digital sunset — screens off 30 min before bed
- [ ] Day review — what went well, what to improve
- [ ] Release — empty your mind onto paper
- [ ] Gratitude close — name one good thing

## Sleep Environment

Optimize your space for rest:

- Keep the room cool and dark
- Use calming scents like lavender
- Reserve your bed only for sleep

> "The way you end each day is the way you begin the next."
